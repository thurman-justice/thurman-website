The Work

I'm a Senior Engineering Manager based in the greater D.C./Baltimore area, currently leading platform modernization and engineering teams at Cigna Healthcare. I've spent the last 12+ years working at the intersection of distributed systems architecture and cross-functional leadership — figuring out how to move fast without breaking things that matter in a regulated, high-stakes environment.


My work sits at a dual track: on one side, designing the systems — event-driven microservices, cloud-native AWS architecture, compliance-grade data pipelines, and the patterns that make them observable and maintainable at scale. On the other, building the teams and practices that deliver them — hiring, onboarding, coaching, and creating the conditions where engineers can do their best work.


Some things I've driven that I'm proud of: moving a legacy monolithic platform to event-driven microservices across 4 teams, replacing a 24-hour batch sync with a self-healing near real-time pipeline serving 1–2M records a month, and being the first in my organization to navigate our Enterprise Cloud Center of Excellence — then building the documentation so everyone who came after had a clearer path.

How I think about Leadership

I believe the job of an engineering leader is to remove friction — from the technical architecture, from the team's workflows, and from the space between engineering and the business. The best systems I've been part of were built by teams who understood why they were building them, not just what they were building.


I care about career development at every level. I've mentored engineers through Cigna's Technology Early Career Development Program and the Diversity Investment Venture (DIVE) program — working with people in engineering-adjacent roles who were building toward a career in technology. That investment matters to me.
